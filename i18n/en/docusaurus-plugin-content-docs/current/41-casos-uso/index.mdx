## Student support

> Teach students to use it well

- "I am a student of... I have to prepare for an exam... can you help me?"
- "Ask me questions similar to these"
- "Rigorously correct the answers, pointing out everything that can be improved."
- "Propose suggestions for style improvement for the following text, making it more academic and improving grammar. The text is:..."
- "Point out the weak points of my work, indicating what I can improve. The work is for the module..."
- "Help me study through outlines. Prepare an outline from the following notes."
- "Generate flashcards to help me memorize the following concepts."



## Fighting chatbots

### Don't put gates on the field

- Normalize working with chatbots instead of chasing them
- They are a new tool that we will all work with, and what they have to do is use it well.
- If they don't use it well, it shows (excess level, poorly planned prompts that lead to errors...)
- If they use it well, they are learning.
- That they mention when they use it and attach the prompts.


- Exam with chatgpt:
- Ask something extremely typical, but change key elements.



### But if we want to audit it...

- Interview with the student

- Ask the AIs themselves. Attach texts from the student himself.
- How possible is it that this text was generated by a chatbot? (ask all the popular ones)
- How possible is it that this text was written by a 2nd year high school student?
- What could I ask a student to find out if they are the author of this work?

- Plagiarism detectors
